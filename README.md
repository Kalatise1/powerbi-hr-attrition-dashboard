# HR Attrition Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

## Project Overview
Employee attrition is one of the most critical challenges organizations face today. High employee turnover increases recruitment costs, disrupts productivity, and negatively affects organizational performance.

This project presents an **interactive HR analytics dashboard built using Power BI** to analyze employee attrition patterns and identify the key drivers behind employee turnover.

Using the **IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset**, this dashboard provides insights into workforce demographics, employee satisfaction, compensation patterns, and factors influencing employee departures.

The goal of this project is to demonstrate how **data analytics can support HR decision-making by identifying attrition risks and workforce trends**.

---

# Dataset

The dataset contains **1470 employee records** with multiple attributes related to workforce demographics, job roles, compensation, and work environment.

Key variables in the dataset include:

- Age  
- Department  
- Job Role  
- Monthly Income  
- Years at Company  
- Job Satisfaction  
- Work Life Balance  
- Overtime  
- Marital Status  
- Attrition (Yes / No)

These variables allow HR teams to analyze **why employees leave organizations and what factors influence attrition**.

# Dashboard Structure

The dashboard is divided into **three main analytical sections**.

---

---

# 2. Attrition Drivers Analysis

This section focuses on identifying **key drivers of employee attrition**.

### Attrition by Overtime
Employees working overtime show **higher attrition levels**, indicating potential work-life balance concerns.

### Attrition by Work-Life Balance
Employees with lower work-life balance scores experience higher attrition.

### Attrition by Job Satisfaction
Lower job satisfaction levels correlate strongly with employee turnover.

### Attrition by Marital Status
Single employees show higher attrition rates compared to married employees.

### Years at Company vs Monthly Income
A scatter plot visualization highlights relationships between tenure, income, and attrition.

This section helps organizations understand **why employees leave**.

---

# 3. Workforce and Compensation Insights

This section focuses on workforce structure and salary patterns.

### Average Income by Job Role
Managers and research roles tend to earn higher salaries, while operational roles such as sales representatives have lower average incomes.

### Average Income by Department
The **Sales department** shows slightly higher average income compared to HR and Research departments.

### Employees by Years at Company
Most employees have **less than 10 years of tenure**, indicating a relatively young workforce.

### Attrition by Department and Job Role
A detailed table provides insights into attrition counts and rates across departments and job roles.

This helps identify **high-risk roles with higher employee turnover**.

---

# Key Insights

Several patterns emerged from the analysis:

- Employees working overtime are more likely to leave.
- Job satisfaction is strongly linked to employee retention.
- Employees with shorter tenure have higher attrition risk.
- Some job roles experience higher turnover than others.
- Salary differences across job roles may influence retention.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the insights generated from this analysis, organizations should consider the following actions:

### Improve Work-Life Balance
Reduce excessive overtime and promote healthier work schedules.

### Strengthen Employee Engagement
Improve job satisfaction through recognition programs, career development, and leadership support.

### Focus on Early Career Retention
Implement stronger onboarding and mentorship programs for newer employees.

### Monitor High-Risk Roles
Departments and job roles with high attrition should receive targeted HR interventions.

### Review Compensation Structures
Salary disparities across roles should be reviewed to ensure competitive compensation.
